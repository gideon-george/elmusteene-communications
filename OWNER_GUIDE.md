# Updating Your Website — Owner's Guide

Your website has a **content manager**: a private page where you can add
laptops, change prices, update your phone numbers or opening hours, and swap
photos — from your phone or a computer, without touching any code.

## Logging in

1. Open **your-site-address/admin** (ask Gideon for the exact link — bookmark
   it or save it to your phone's home screen).
2. Tap **Sign In with GitHub** and log in with the GitHub account that was
   set up for you.
3. That's it — you'll see your content organised in sections on the left.

> Every time you press **Save/Publish**, the live site updates itself within
> about 2 minutes. Refresh the site to see your change.

## Post a new laptop for sale

1. Open **Laptops for sale** → **New Laptop**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Name** — only what a customer can see: "Dell laptop", "HP laptop".
   - **Photo** — tap to upload a clear photo of the exact unit.
   - **Photo description** — one sentence describing the photo.
   - **Specs** *(optional)* — only if you are sure, e.g.
     `Intel Core i5 · 8GB RAM · 256GB SSD`. If you leave it empty the card
     says "Specs & price on request".
   - **Price** *(optional)* — e.g. `₦450,000`. Empty = price on request.
   - **Display order** — lower numbers show first.
3. Press **Save**. Done — it's in the gallery.

When a laptop is **sold**, open its entry and delete it.

## Change phone numbers, hours or address

Open **Site settings** → **Business info**. Type phone numbers plainly, like
`07065010455` — the site formats them and wires up the Call and WhatsApp
buttons automatically. Hours are plain text too (`8:00 AM`, `7:00 PM`).

## Edit services or photos

- **Site settings → Services** — the wording, bullet points, photos and
  WhatsApp messages for each service.
- **Site settings → Photo galleries** — the About page photos and the bulk
  stock photos on the Laptops page.

## Three honest-content rules

1. **Never invent specs or prices.** Empty is better than wrong — the site
   then invites customers to ask on WhatsApp.
2. **Photo descriptions matter.** Describe what's really in the photo — it
   helps customers using screen readers and helps Google find you.
3. **Use your own photos.** Photos from the internet may be watermarked or
   copyrighted.

## If something goes wrong

Every change is saved in the site's history, so nothing is ever truly lost —
message Gideon and any change can be undone.
